Before I get into that I want to tell you about a paradox from Zeno of Elea. The conundrum goes, if a man shoots an arrow at an apple, it will never hit it. This is because before it can strike the apple it must cover half the distance to it, then half the remaining distance, and so on. Mathematically this is the sum of y=1/2^x from 1 to infinity for x. In all it must move an infinite number of distances, and so it shouldn’t ever reach its mark. The resolution to this paradox is that each step only takes half the time as the previous, with the final step taking zero time. This paradox is reversible to show that motion altogether is impossible, as even the slightest motion takes an infinite number of moves.

This inverse of Zeno’s Paradox is loosely related to the feedback loop produced from the fact that “nothing is twice itself”: x=/=2*x or 0=0+0=(0+0)+(0+0)…=0*infinity=undefined (or 1). I studied this for a long time before one night at my apartment I heard a voice like a thought come with a pop in my ear. It said “golden oval” which I at once identified as a pun on “good and evil” and to which I completed “golden oval’s red frets” for the red fruits of The Tree of Good and Evil. I drew a yellow oval and added red radial lines along its perimeter. I spaced the “frets” evenly for the evenness of gold numbers (Fibonacci numbers) and red numbers (non-Fibonacci numbers) of the Fibonacci loop initiated with two zeroes instead of ones. The Fibonacci loop adds the last two values to get a new value. Its first few numbers are 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, and 13. Notice 1+1=2, 1+2=3, 2+3=5 and so on. With 0, and 0 as the first two members all you get is more 0's. Since 0 and 1 are sometimes used to start the Fibonacci sequence, 0 is both a member and not a member.
