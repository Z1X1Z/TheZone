  • And now these three remain: faith, hope and love. But the greatest of these is love.

    1 Corinthians 13:13

    St. Patrick compared the Holy Trinity to a clover. It's said the leaves of a four leaf clover stand for faith, hope, love, and grace. It's also porported that the fifth and sixth leaves may be wealth (and you should know that spiritual riches are the most valuable kind) and lastly, life (given that there are an infinite number of six leafed clovers, it does seem it would be the eternal kind). I've heard it said that the clover analogy fails to represent the sameness of the three persons of the Trinity. To reconcile this you could make a spinner out of two three leafed clovers, where each person is one leaf of both clovers such that when you spin them, it connects Father to Son, Son to Spirit, Spirit to Father.
